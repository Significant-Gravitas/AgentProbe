# Staff Backend Engineer (Infrastructure & Core Services)

## About Quill
Quill is a Series B SaaS platform building the next generation of collaborative intelligence tools. We process billions of events daily and are currently re-architecting our core data ingestion and real-time synchronization engine. We are looking for a Staff Backend Engineer to lead the design and implementation of our high-throughput distributed systems.

## The Role
As a Staff Backend Engineer, you will own the architectural roadmap for our core services. You won't just be writing features; you will be ensuring our system can scale 10x while maintaining 99.99% availability. You will mentor senior engineers, drive technical excellence, and make foundational technology choices.

### Key Responsibilities
- Lead the migration of legacy Ruby/Node services into high-performance Go and Rust microservices.
- Design and implement distributed systems capable of handling massive concurrency and low-latency data synchronization.
- Optimize database performance (PostgreSQL) and caching layers (Redis) at scale.
- Architect event-driven systems using Kafka or NATS.
- Set the standard for observability, testing, and deployment patterns across the engineering organization.

## Requirements
- **Experience:** 8+ years of professional software engineering experience, with at least 3 years in a staff or principal capacity.
- **Languages:** Expert-level proficiency in **Go** or **Rust**. We are moving heavily toward these for performance-critical paths.
- **Systems:** Deep understanding of distributed systems primitives (consensus algorithms, CAP theorem, partitioning, replication).
- **Cloud/Infra:** Experience with Kubernetes, Terraform, and AWS/GCP.
- **Data:** Strong experience with SQL and NoSQL internals; experience with stream processing (Flink, Spark) is a plus.
- **Communication:** Ability to articulate complex technical trade-offs to both technical and non-technical stakeholders.
- **Location:** Remote (US or EU time zones).

## Our Tech Stack
- **Backend:** Go, Rust, some legacy Node.js/Ruby
- **Infrastructure:** AWS, Kubernetes (EKS), Terraform
- **Data:** PostgreSQL, Redis, Kafka, ClickHouse
- **CI/CD:** GitHub Actions, ArgoCD
