# Course Syllabus: ADV-FS 402 - Principles of Food Microbiology & Biotechnology

## Course Description
An advanced exploration of the microbial ecosystems within food processing, preservation, and safety. This course bridging the gap between basic microbiology and industrial application.

## Completed Modules (Weeks 1-8)

### Module 1: Microbial Physiology and Growth Kinetics
*   **The Bacterial Cell Wall:** Differences between Gram-positive and Gram-negative structures in foodborne pathogens.
*   **Growth Curves:** Lag, log, stationary, and death phases; calculating doubling times in specific food matrices.
*   **Environmental Stress Factors:** Impact of pH, water activity (aw), redox potential (Eh), and temperature on microbial survival.

### Module 2: Food Preservation Technologies
*   **Thermal Processing:** D-values, z-values, and F-values; pasteurization vs. sterilization.
*   **Non-Thermal Methods:** High-pressure processing (HPP), pulsed electric fields (PEF), and UV irradiation.
*   **Chemical Preservatives:** Organic acids, nitrates/nitrites, and the "clean label" movement in antimicrobial selection.

### Module 3: Traditional Fermentation Pathways
*   **Lactic Acid Bacteria (LAB):** Homofermentative vs. heterofermentative pathways; the role of *Lactobacillus*, *Lactococcus*, and *Pediococcus*.
*   **Alcoholic Fermentation:** *Saccharomyces cerevisiae* metabolism and ethanol tolerance.
*   **Acetic Acid & Alkaline Fermentations:** Production of vinegars and fermented soy products (natto).

### Module 4: Foodborne Pathogens and Toxins
*   **Infection vs. Intoxication:** Detailed study of *Salmonella*, *Listeria monocytogenes*, and *Clostridium botulinum*.
*   **Mycotoxins:** Aflatoxins and ochratoxins in grain storage.
*   **HACCP & FSMA:** Regulatory frameworks for ensuring microbial safety in the US and EU.

### Module 5: Introduction to Genomics and Sequencing
*   **16S rRNA Sequencing:** Principles of phylogenetic identification.
*   **Metagenomics vs. Metatranscriptomics:** Understanding the difference between "who is there" and "what they are doing."

## Current Learning Objectives (Pre-Lecture Status)
By the end of this week, students should transition from understanding fermentation as a **preservation technique** to understanding it as a **functional health tool**.

**Prerequisite Knowledge for Upcoming Lecture:**
1.  Students are expected to be familiar with the "Probiotic" definition (FAO/WHO).
2.  Students must understand the basics of the human GI tract anatomy.
3.  Students should recall the metabolic end-products of LAB (Lactic Acid, CO2, bacteriocins).
