# Project: DuoHabit - Shared Habit Tracking for Couples

## Core Concept
DuoHabit is a mobile-first web application designed specifically for couples who want to build and maintain habits together. Unlike traditional habit trackers that are built for individuals or large social groups, DuoHabit focuses on the unique psychology of romantic partnerships—leveraging mutual accountability, shared rewards, and synchronized progress.

The core hypothesis is that couples are more likely to stick to a habit (e.g., "no screens after 10 PM" or "daily 15-minute walk") if their progress is visually and functionally linked to their partner's, creating a "we're in this together" feedback loop rather than a solo streak.

## Value Proposition
- **Synchronized Streaks:** A single "Shared Streak" that only increments if both partners complete the habit.
- **Micro-Nudges:** Non-intrusive ways to remind a partner without the friction of a text message.
- **Shared Rewards:** A system to unlock "Date Night" ideas or shared incentives when milestones are reached.
- **Privacy-First Accountability:** High-level visibility into partner progress without invasive monitoring.

*   **Time Commitment:** Maximum of 10 hours per week. This includes development, design, and any initial outreach.
*   **Budget:** $0 initial capital. I will use free-tier services only (Vercel, Supabase, Tailwind CSS, GitHub Actions). No paid marketing.
*   **Timeline:** The MVP must be fully functional and "ship-ready" within an 8-week development cycle.
*   **Technical Stack:** Must leverage my existing proficiency in React/Next.js to avoid a learning curve.
*   **Success Definition:** 10 active "couples" (20 users) who maintain a 7-day shared streak within the first month of launch.

## Target User
The "Intentional Couple": Ages 25–40, living together or in a long-term committed relationship, who already use productivity tools (Notion, Google Calendar) but find individual habit trackers isolating or redundant for shared lifestyle goals.

## Key Features for Consideration
1.  **Dual-Auth Onboarding:** A simple way to invite a partner via a unique link that bridges two accounts.
2.  **The "Shared Wall":** A dashboard showing the status of 2-3 high-impact shared habits.
3.  **Completion Triggers:** When Partner A completes a task, Partner B receives a celebratory notification.
4.  **The "Slacker" Grace Period:** A mechanism to handle days where one partner genuinely cannot complete the habit without breaking the "Shared Streak" (e.g., illness).

## Goal for this Research Phase
I need to identify if existing tools (Habitica, Streaks, etc.) already solve the "mutual accountability" problem effectively or if their "social" features are too generic for the intimacy of a couple. I am looking for the specific "friction point" in current apps that prevents couples from using them together.
