# Linear Brand Voice & Style Guide

## Core Philosophy
Linear is built for high-performance teams. Our voice reflects our product: it is purposeful, precise, and opinionated. We don't use fluff to fill space. We don't use "marketing-speak" to hide a lack of substance. We speak to engineers and designers as peers, not as targets.

## The Pillars

### 1. Opinionated, Not Arrogant
We believe there is a "right way" to build software. We aren't afraid to say that backlogs are where ideas go to die or that excessive meetings are a symptom of broken process.
- **Do:** Take a stand. "Cycles are better than sprints."
- **Don't:** Be condescending. Avoid "You're doing it wrong." Instead, use "We've found a better way."

### 2. Minimalist and Dense
Every word must earn its place. If a sentence can be shorter without losing meaning, shorten it. We value the reader's time.
- **Do:** "Focus is a feature."
- **Don't:** "One of the most important things we prioritize here at Linear is the ability for our users to maintain a high level of focus."

### 3. Engineer-Respectful
Our audience is technically literate and has a high "BS detector." Avoid buzzwords (synergy, disruption, game-changing). Use precise terminology.
- **Do:** Discuss "git-integrated workflows," "latency," and "keyboard shortcuts."
- **Don't:** Use vague metaphors about "accelerating your digital transformation journey."

### 4. Low-Entropy Aesthetics
Our writing should feel like our UI: clean, dark-mode friendly (metaphorically), and high-contrast. Use active verbs. Avoid passive voice.

## Channel-Specific Application

### Twitter (X)
- **Structure:** Start with a declarative statement. Use threads to break down complex methodologies into atomic units.
- **Tone:** Sharp, punchy, slightly provocative.
- **Visuals:** Use code snippets or UI screenshots to ground the theory in reality.

### LinkedIn
- **Structure:** Longer-form reflection. Start with a contrarian observation about industry standards.
- **Tone:** Professional but weary of "hustle culture." Focus on the craft of building.
- **CTA:** Single, clear, and low-friction.

### Video/Audio
- **Pacing:** Fast but deliberate. No "Hey guys, welcome back to the channel."
- **Hook:** Immediate value or a direct challenge to the status quo.
- **Closing:** No "smash that like button." Direct them to the Method or the product.

## The "Linear Method" Lexicon
- **Cycles:** Not sprints. Cycles are about momentum, not just deadlines.
- **Issues:** Not tickets. Tickets are for support; issues are for builders.
- **Roadmaps:** High-level direction, not a rigid 12-month promise.
- **Quality:** A baseline requirement, not a "nice to have" in a backlog.

## Prohibited Terms
- "Revolutionary"
- "User-friendly" (Show, don't tell)
- "Synergy"
- "Agile" (when used as a generic corporate buzzword)
- "Deep dive" (unless it's actually deep)
